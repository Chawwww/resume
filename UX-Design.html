Malayan Tiger

Panthera tigris jacksoni

Critically Endangered

Description

The Malayan tiger is a tiger subspecies that inhabits the southern and central parts of the Malay Peninsula. It is one of the smallest tiger subspecies, with males averaging 2.4 m in length.

Conservation Status

Less than 200 individuals remain in the wild, making it critically endangered. Major threats include habitat loss, poaching, and human-wildlife conflict.

Habitat

Tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests of the Malaysian Peninsula.

About SDG 15: Life on Land

Sustainable Forests

SDG 15 aims to protect, restore and promote sustainable use of terrestrial ecosystems, sustainably manage forests, combat desertification, and halt and reverse land degradation and biodiversity loss.

Wildlife Conservation

Take urgent and significant action to reduce the degradation of natural habitats, halt the loss of biodiversity and, by 2025, protect and prevent the extinction of threatened species.

Community Action

Mobilize and significantly increase financial resources from all sources to conserve and sustainably use biodiversity and ecosystems through community engagement and partnerships.

LifeOnLand

Dedicated to protecting terrestrial ecosystems and biodiversity through education, awareness, and action.
